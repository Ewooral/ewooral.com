Where We Are

A technically excellent product with zero market validation. We've built Phases
1-4 (MVP, automation, intelligence, monetization) before acquiring a single  
paying customer. This is a common founder trap — building in a vacuum.

The product brief says the target is 20 salons onboarded, 10 paying, GH₵ 2,000
MRR in 3 months. We're at 0/0/0.

The Core Problem

We don't know if salon owners in Accra will pay GH₵ 80-600/month for this. We
think they will because the product brief says so. But thinking isn't knowing.
Every assumption — pricing, features, onboarding flow, even the name "Ahofe" —
is untested.

The Strategy: 10 Customers in 30 Days

Not 1,000. Not 100. 10 real salon owners using the product daily. Everything
else is premature.

Phase 1: Weeks 1-2 — Manual Sales (Zero Budget)

No ads. No SEO. No content marketing. Those are scaling tools. You scale what
works. We don't know what works yet.

Action: Elijah walks into 20 salons in East Legon / Osu / Accra Mall area.

The pitch (30 seconds, in-person):

▎ "I built a free app that lets your customers book appointments from WhatsApp.
▎ You get a booking link, they pick a time — no more phone calls. It also tracks
▎  your revenue. Can I show you on your phone? Takes 2 minutes."

Why in-person:

* Salon owners in Accra don't read email campaigns
* They trust people, not websites
* You see their real reaction — confusion, excitement, objections
* You learn what matters to them (hint: it might not be what we built)

What you're actually doing: Market research disguised as sales. You're not
selling software — you're answering:

1. Do they understand the value proposition in 30 seconds?
2. What's their first objection? (price? complexity? "I don't need this"?)
3. Do they already use something? (paper? WhatsApp groups? nothing?)
4. Would they share the booking link on their WhatsApp status?

Target: 20 conversations → 10 sign-ups → 5 active users in 2 weeks.

Phase 2: Weeks 3-4 — Activate \& Learn

For the 5-10 who signed up:

1. Personally onboard each one. Sit with them. Add their services. Add their
stylists. Set their hours. Share the booking link on THEIR WhatsApp status while
you're there.
2. WhatsApp group with all early users. Daily check-in: "How's the app working?
Any bookings today?" This is your feedback loop.
3. Track obsessively:
- How many bookings per salon per week?
- Are customers actually using the booking link?
- What feature do they open most?
- What feature do they never touch?
- Did anyone share the link organically?
4. Fix what's broken in real-time. The backend and frontend agents can ship
daily. If a salon owner says "I can't see tomorrow's bookings easily" — that's a
feature request with a real face behind it.

Phase 3: Month 2 — First Revenue

Once 5+ salons are active and booking through the app:

1. Don't charge yet. Keep them on Free tier. The goal is daily active usage, not
revenue.
2. Ask the magic question: "If this app disappeared tomorrow, would you miss
it?" If yes — you have product-market fit for that user. If no — ask why not.
3. When 3+ salons say they'd miss it, introduce the Starter tier (GH₵ 80/month)
with a pitch: "You've been using the free version. The paid version sends
automatic reminders to your customers so they don't forget their appointment.
Want to try it for a month?"
4. First paying customer = everything changes. Now you know someone values this
enough to pay. Now you can start thinking about growth.

Phase 4: Month 3 — Referral Engine

Salon owners know other salon owners. The cheapest acquisition channel in Ghana:

1. Referral incentive: "Refer a friend, both get 1 month free on Starter."
2. WhatsApp Status marketing kit: Give each salon owner a pre-made WhatsApp
Status image: "Book your appointment online — no calls needed 📱" with their
booking link. They post it, their customers see it.
3. "Powered by Ahofe" on the booking page — every customer who books sees the
brand.

The Honest Truth

The product is exceptional. Three agents, 319 tests, Phases 1-4 built, AI
features, Paystack billing, WhatsApp integration. But none of that matters until
someone uses it.

The next milestone isn't a feature, a commit, or a test. It's Elijah walking
into a salon in East Legon and saying "Can I show you something?"

Everything after that first conversation is execution. And we're very good at
execution.
